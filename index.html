What is humidity??

Humidity is the amount of water vapor present in the air. It is often expressed as a percentage, representing the ratio of the actual water vapor in the air to the maximum amount it could hold at a given temperature. High humidity indicates more moisture in the air, while low humidity indicates less. Humidity influences weather conditions, comfort levels, and various natural processes.


What is Wind-degree??

Wind degree, also known as wind direction, indicates the compass direction from which the wind is blowing. It is measured in degrees, typically ranging from 0° to 360°, with 0° representing a northward direction. For example, a wind degree of 180° means the wind is coming from the south. Wind direction is a crucial element in weather forecasts and is often reported alongside wind speed to provide a comprehensive understanding of atmospheric conditions.


What is Wind-Speed??

Wind speed is a measure of how fast air is moving horizontally past a particular point. It is typically expressed in units such as meters per second (m/s) or miles per hour (mph). Wind speed is a key component of weather conditions and is often used in forecasts to describe the intensity of the wind. Higher wind speeds generally indicate more powerful and potentially impactful weather conditions, while lower wind speeds suggest calmer atmospheric conditions.
